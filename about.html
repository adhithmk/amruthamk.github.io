Assistant Professor | Researcher | Digital Humanist

Research Approach

My research is characterized by an interdisciplinary approach that bridges traditional humanities scholarship with digital methodologies. I am particularly interested in how digital tools can help us ask new questions about cultural texts and historical narratives, especially those from postcolonial contexts that have been historically underrepresented in digital spaces.

My work often involves close textual analysis, digital text encoding, archival research, and the development of digital projects that make humanities research more accessible to broader audiences. I am committed to open-access scholarship and the ethical application of digital methods in humanities research.
